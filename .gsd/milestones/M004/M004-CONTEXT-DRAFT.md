# M004: SaaS Hardening and Commercialization — Context Draft

**Gathered:** 2026-03-17
**Status:** Draft — needs dedicated discussion before planning

## Seed From Current Discussion

M004 is the milestone where the project should evolve from a strong local-first intelligence tool into a more industrialized SaaS product.

The user wants the product to become commercial eventually, but without sacrificing the property that gives it value: credibility. The radar must stay evidence-backed, explicit about uncertainty, and analytically serious even if the surface becomes more polished, richer, and more market-ready.

The user also wants a strong, beautiful SaaS-style design over time — comfortable, effective, visually rich where it helps — but not at the expense of analytical usefulness.

## What This Milestone Likely Advances

- SaaS-grade durability, polish, and product hardening
- stronger design system and more commercial-grade user experience
- operationalization beyond a single local operator
- packaging the radar as a product other people can use without losing evidence and trust surfaces
- preparing commercialization paths while preserving the observed / inferred / uncertain contract

## Candidate User-Visible Outcome

When this milestone is complete, the product should feel like a real SaaS offering rather than an advanced prototype:

- more durable and polished
- easier to operate at product scale
- commercially presentable
- still grounded in evidence, confidence, and explainability

## Candidate Capabilities

- account, tenancy, and hosted product concerns if needed
- stronger operability, observability, and lifecycle management
- production-oriented deployment and runtime hardening
- design, interaction, and product polish improvements suitable for a commercial SaaS
- commercial packaging features that do not compromise analytical integrity

## Constraints To Preserve

- the product must not become visually rich but analytically weak
- credibility must remain the anchor even as the product becomes more commercial
- uncertainty, confidence, and evidence linkage must stay visible enough to preserve trust
- commercialization should not push the product into shallow “AI dashboard” territory

## Why This Is Drafted Instead Of Finalized Now

A dedicated M004 discussion should happen later because it depends on what the product actually becomes through M001–M003:

- how much of the local architecture survives into hosted operation
- which product surfaces matter most to real users
- what commercialization path is actually intended
- how much product polish and operational hardening is already achieved earlier

## Open Questions For Future Discussion

- What is the first real commercial packaging of the product?
- Which SaaS hardening concerns matter first: hosting, accounts, billing, team use, reliability, observability, or support workflows?
- How should the product balance beautiful design with evidence density and analytical clarity?
- Which parts of the current local-first workflow should remain visible to advanced users versus hidden behind a SaaS experience?
